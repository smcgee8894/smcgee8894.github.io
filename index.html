Experimental Methods Project

The goal of this project was to design and carry out a scientific experiment using the equipment available in NAU's thermal fluids laboratory. My proposal involved measuring the temperature profile of a heat sink placed on a hot plate after reaching steady-state conditions. Twelve K-type thermocouples were calibrated and installed along various surfaces of the heat sink, whereupon temperature data was captured and stored. Experimental results were then compared to results from MATLAB and Ansys simulations. Both simulations delivered results within ±5°C of the experimental results.

  • Designed experimental procedures to minimize impact of external factors such as forced convection
  • Collected data using LabVIEW and the NI-9213 temperature input module
  • Analyzed uncertainty of results to establish confidence interval
  • Performed 3D steady state thermal analyses using Ansys and MATLAB PDE Toolbox
  • Detailed experimental setup and procedures, simulation techniques, and results in a professional report
